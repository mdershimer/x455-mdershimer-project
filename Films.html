Films

I made this film as a submission to the San Francisco Museum of Fine Art’s local artists show at the DeYoung Museum. The theme was “on the edge,” which I used as a literal boundary for the image in my film, only showing the images on the edges of a square around a center square box holding the text. The film talks about my interest in watching the moonrise, starting from a scientific magazine article I read in college, paintings I made from my moon watches there, and my move to the Bay Area and how I was worried about no longer being able to watch the moon as well.

This is an 8mm film that my father made as a child in the 1970s. It uses a combination of stop-motion techniques and live action to tell the story of an animal landing on a foreign planet and the dangers he encounters there, including alien life. I had a friend who works at a film archive digitize the film for me and color-corrected it as best I could.

This is a fun film I made of myself and my step-brother on the Flying Horses Carousel on the island of Martha’s Vineyard off the coast of Cape Cod in Massachusetts. It is a rare brass ring carousel, where they have standard pewter rings that you can practice grabbing as you ride by, and one golden brass ring that comes out last, and if you catch it you win a free ride. I have been riding the carousel since I was a little girl, and have gotten pretty good at grabbing multiple rings at once. I noticed that while there were a lot of video clips of the carousel on YouTube, there were very few representations of people catching multiple rings at once. My mom recorded these clips of us riding and I edited them with slow motion to show the technique.
